üìà Should I Raise My Price?

Are you selling everything you make?

Do customers ever say "That's a great deal!"?

Have your supply costs gone up recently?

‚úÖ Yes, Raise Your Price!

Based on your answers, you have room to increase prices. Here's how:

  • Try a 10-20% increase first
  • Improve packaging when you raise prices
  • Announce it positively: "New and improved!"
  • Watch if sales stay steady - if yes, you made the right call!

ü§î Maybe - Test It First

You might be able to raise prices, but test carefully:

  • Try raising on just ONE product first
  • See how customers react for a few weeks
  • If sales stay same, consider raising others

‚ö†Ô∏è Probably Not Right Now

Your prices seem appropriate for now. Focus on:

  • Selling more volume instead
  • Finding ways to reduce your costs
  • Adding value to justify higher prices later

üí° General Price Raising Tips

  • It's easier to raise prices on NEW customers
  • Give loyal customers a heads-up
  • Add something new when you raise (better packaging, extra item)
  • Don't apologize - be confident!

üè∑Ô∏è Should I Offer a Discount?

Why do you want to offer a discount?

ü§î Discounts Rarely Fix Slow Sales

If people aren't buying, the problem usually isn't price. Try these first:

  • Better marketing (tell more people!)
  • Improve your product presentation
  • Sell in different locations
  • Make sure people know you exist!

‚úÖ Yes, Clear That Stock!

Discounting old or leftover inventory makes sense:

  • Better to sell cheap than throw away
  • Mark it clearly as "clearance" or "last chance"
  • Don't discount new products at the same time

ü§î Be Careful with "Loss Leaders"

Discounting to attract customers can work, but:

  • Only if you have other things to upsell
  • Make it a LIMITED time offer
  • Don't train customers to always expect discounts

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Discount Just Because Asked

Standing firm on your prices shows confidence:

  • Your prices are based on real costs
  • You can offer value instead: "I can add a sample!"
  • Or bundle: "If you buy 3, I'll make it $X"
  • It's okay if they walk away - not everyone is your customer

üí° Smart Discounting Rules

  • Never discount more than 20% (protect your profit!)
  • Make discounts rare and special
  • Bundle instead of discount: "Buy 2 get 1 free"
  • Calculate if you can still profit at discount price

üì¶ Should I Bundle Products?

‚úÖ Bundles Are Usually Great!

Bundling is one of the best pricing strategies. Here's why it works:

  • Customers feel like they're getting a deal
  • You sell more per transaction
  • Great for gift-giving occasions
  • Helps you use up slower-selling items

üí° Bundle Ideas

  • Quantity bundles: "3 for $15" (instead of $6 each)
  • Variety packs: "Try all 3 flavors for $20"
  • Gift sets: Multiple items in a nice box
  • Starter kits: Everything they need to get started
  • Price bundles 10-15% less than items would cost separately

üèÉ Competitor Is Cheaper!

üòå Don't Panic! Here's What to Do

Being more expensive than competitors isn't always bad:

üéØ Option 1: Differentiate

  • What makes YOUR product special?
  • Better ingredients? Handmade? Local?
  • Unique flavors or designs?
  • Better packaging or presentation?
  • Communicate this! People pay more for quality.

üéØ Option 2: Find Your Customers

  • Not everyone shops on price
  • Sell where quality matters more
  • Target customers who appreciate handmade
  • Let cheap competitors have the bargain hunters

üéØ Option 3: Add Value

  • Include something extra they don't
  • Better customer service
  • Custom options or personalization
  • Loyalty rewards for repeat customers

‚ö†Ô∏è What NOT To Do

  • Don't just lower your prices to match
  • Don't badmouth the competition
  • Don't panic and give up!

üìâ Should I Lower My Price?

Why are you thinking about lowering your price?

‚ö†Ô∏è Price Probably Isn't the Problem

Not selling usually means a marketing or location problem, not pricing:

  • Are enough people seeing your product?
  • Is your presentation appealing?
  • Are you selling in the right place?
  • Try fixing these BEFORE lowering price

ü§î Consider the Source

Before lowering, ask yourself:

  • Are MANY people saying this, or just a few?
  • Are these your target customers?
  • Can you add value instead of lowering?
  • Is your price based on real costs?

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Race to the Bottom

Competing on price is a losing game:

  • Someone can always go cheaper
  • Focus on being BETTER, not cheaper
  • Find customers who value quality
  • Differentiate your product instead

‚úÖ This Is the Right Reason!

If your costs went down, you could:

  • Keep prices same and enjoy more profit
  • Lower slightly to attract more customers
  • Use savings to improve packaging/quality

üí° Before Lowering Any Price

  • Calculate if you'll still profit
  • Remember: selling MORE at lower profit might mean more work for less money
  • Consider: can you add a smaller/cheaper option instead?
  • Once you lower, it's hard to raise again
