Not Selling Anything

üîç Common Causes:

  • Not enough people know about your product
  • Selling in the wrong place or at the wrong time
  • Product doesn't look appealing
  • Price might be too high (or too low!)
  • Not explaining the product well

üí° Solutions to Try:

üì¢ Get the Word Out

More people need to know you exist! You can't sell if nobody knows about you.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Tell EVERYONE you know - family, friends, neighbors
  2. Ask parents to share on their social media
  3. Make simple flyers and post in allowed spots
  4. Offer free samples to get people talking
üìç Change Your Location

You might be trying to sell in a spot where your customers aren't.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Think about where YOUR customers hang out
  2. Try a different day or time
  3. Consider a farmers market or school event
  4. Sell online through parents' social media
‚ú® Improve Your Presentation

People buy with their eyes first! Make your product look amazing.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Upgrade your packaging (it doesn't have to be expensive)
  2. Take better photos with good lighting
  3. Create a nice display for in-person sales
  4. Add labels or tags to look more professional

üí™ Remember:

Every successful business had slow days! Try ONE new thing at a time so you can see what works. Give each change at least a week or two before trying something else.

People Say It's Too Expensive

üîç First, Ask Yourself:

  • IS your price actually too high? (Check competitors)
  • Are you selling to the WRONG audience?
  • Do customers understand WHY it's worth the price?
  • Are they just trying to negotiate?

üí° Solutions to Try:

üéØ Find the RIGHT Customers

Not everyone is your customer! Some people will never pay for quality - find those who will.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Sell at places where people spend more (farmers markets, boutiques)
  2. Target people who already buy similar products
  3. Don't waste time on price-complainers
üíé Show Your Value

Help people understand WHY your product is worth it!

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Explain what makes your product special (handmade, organic, local)
  2. Share the time and love that goes into making it
  3. Compare to store prices for similar quality
  4. Let them taste/try samples
üì¶ Offer Options

Give people choices at different price points.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Create a smaller/cheaper option
  2. Bundle items for better value ("3 for $15")
  3. Offer a "budget" line alongside premium

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Just Lower Your Price!

If your price is based on real costs, lowering it means losing money. It's better to find customers who value quality than to race to the bottom!

Not Making Profit

üîç Common Causes:

  • Price is too low for your costs
  • Hidden costs you're not counting
  • Not counting your time as a cost
  • Giving too many discounts or freebies
  • Supply costs went up but price didn't

üí° Solutions to Try:

üßÆ Recalculate Everything

Go back to basics and add up ALL your costs.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Use our Pricing Calculator to recalculate
  2. Include ALL supplies (even small things!)
  3. Add your time at $10-15/hour minimum
  4. Count packaging, labels, bags
  5. Add any selling fees or booth costs
üìà Raise Your Prices

Yes, it can feel scary, but it's often necessary!

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Calculate what price you NEED to profit
  2. Raise prices gradually if needed
  3. Improve packaging when raising prices
  4. Announce it positively: "New premium packaging!"
‚úÇÔ∏è Cut Costs

Find ways to make things cheaper without losing quality.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Buy supplies in bulk when possible
  2. Find cheaper supply sources
  3. Reduce packaging costs (but keep it nice!)
  4. Make larger batches to save time

üìä The Rule of 2x:

Your selling price should be AT LEAST 2x your total costs (including your time). If you're below that, you're working for free or losing money!

Keep Running Out of Product

üîç This is Actually GOOD News!

  • It means people want what you're selling!
  • You might be able to sell even more
  • You just need better planning

üí° Solutions to Try:

üìã Track Your Sales

Know how much you're selling so you can plan ahead.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Write down how much you sell each time
  2. Notice patterns (busy days, slow days)
  3. Plan to make more than you think you need
üóìÔ∏è Make a Production Schedule

Plan when you'll make products each week.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Set specific days/times to make products
  2. Make in bigger batches
  3. Stock up before busy times (holidays!)
üí∞ Consider Raising Prices

If you can't keep up with demand, your price might be too low!

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Higher prices = same money with less work
  2. Test a small price increase
  3. If everything still sells, you priced it right!

‚ú® Pro Move:

Take pre-orders! Let customers order in advance so you know exactly how much to make. This is how real businesses handle high demand!

Not Enough Time

üí° Solutions to Try:

üìÜ Schedule Business Time

Block out specific times just for your business.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Pick 2-3 set times per week for your business
  2. Put it on your calendar like homework
  3. Protect that time - no excuses!
‚ö° Work More Efficiently

Get more done in less time.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Make bigger batches at once
  2. Prep ingredients/supplies in advance
  3. Create an assembly line process
  4. Do similar tasks together
üéØ Sell Less, Earn Same

Raise prices so you make the same money with fewer items.

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Raise your prices by 20-30%
  2. You'll sell fewer but earn similar profit
  3. More time for school and fun!

üéí Remember:

School comes first! A successful business is one that fits your life, not takes it over. It's okay to scale back during busy school times and ramp up during breaks.

Bored or Burned Out

üí° Solutions to Try:

üåà Add Something New

Create a new product variation or flavor to get excited again!

üò¥ Take a Break

It's okay to pause! Take a week or two off to recharge.

üîÑ Pivot to Something New

Maybe this product isn't right for you anymore. Try something different!

üéØ Set a Fun Goal

Give yourself something exciting to work toward - a money goal, a new product launch, etc.

üíö Self-Care First:

Burnout is real! If you're not having fun anymore, take a break. The best businesses are run by happy entrepreneurs!

Dealing with Competition

üí° Solutions to Try:

‚ú® Differentiate Yourself

Make YOUR product unique and special!

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Add a unique flavor, color, or feature
  2. Better packaging or presentation
  3. Tell YOUR story - people buy from people!
  4. Offer something they don't (customization, etc.)
üéØ Find Your Niche

Don't compete head-to-head - carve out your own space.

ü§ù Collaborate, Don't Compete

Maybe you can work together or refer customers to each other!

üìà Competition = Validation:

If others are selling the same thing, it means there's a real market! Focus on being the BEST version, not the only option.

üëé

Handling Negative Feedback

üí° Solutions to Try:

üëÇ Listen and Learn

Bad feedback hurts, but it can help you improve!

ACTION STEPS:
  1. Take a deep breath - don't react right away
  2. Is there truth in the feedback?
  3. What can you do differently?
  4. Thank them for helping you improve
üîß Fix the Problem

If it's a real issue, make it right!

üí™ Don't Take It Personally

Sometimes people are just having a bad day. One negative doesn't define you!
